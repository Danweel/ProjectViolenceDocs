# Code of Conduct

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender or sex
identity and expression, orientation, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,  nationality, personal appearance, culture, sub-culture or religion. This includes any harassment of furries and fans of anthropomorphic media.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our
community include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people. 'Toxic empathy' is encouraged.
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences. This is not the space for debates, regardless of perceived moral standing.
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback - feedback that is clear, instructive and based in technique.
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes. This requires listening to those affected, even if you don't believe it should be considered. Consider it anyway, preferably away from the confrontation/conversation which can cloud our judgement.
* Focusing on what is best not just for one individual, but for all individuals in the community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind. We are sex and kink-positive, but this space is intended to be SFW, safe for minors, and free of behaviors that could make anyone uncomfortable for thier own reasons.
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks. This includes 'joking' behavior that could be mis-understood as such. Apologies must be made to an affected individual.
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical address, age, email address, or other identifiable information without their explicit permission (which we will have to see.)
* A rule of thumb is any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting. If you are not sure, contact a moderator in our Discord.
* These guidelines are to outline the spirit of the law, not the letter. Skirting these guidelines or going up to the line will be seen as 'bad faith'.

**TL;DR**
* **If you're nice to people, you'll be okay.** 🐱

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when
an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at
